# Legal Playbook

Version: 1.0
Owner: Legal Counsel (demo)
Applies to: legal threats, regulator mentions, data privacy rights requests, high risk public communications, fraud allegations at scale

## 1) Mission
Reduce legal and regulatory exposure, preserve evidence, and approve safe language for external communications.

## 2) Inputs From Dashboard
- incident evidence quotes and timestamps
- risk indicators (compliance sensitive, legal threat)
- draft reverse prompt output for review

## 3) Core Rules
- Avoid admissions of fault.
- Avoid definitive statements without evidence.
- Preserve evidence and maintain chain of custody.
- Route potential suspicious activity through the appropriate internal reporting path.

## 4) Legal Triage SOP
### Step 1: Categorize
- legal threat (lawsuit, police, regulator)
- defamation or harmful content
- data privacy request (access, deletion, correction)
- fraud allegation with reputational impact
- contract dispute (business customer)

### Step 2: Preserve evidence
- save screenshots, links, logs references
- record who captured it and when

### Step 3: Provide approved language
- short, cautious, non committal
- directs customer to official channels
- avoids legal conclusions

### Step 4: Decide escalation
- Management for P0 and reputational risk
- IT for security and data incidents
- Finance for financial remediation review
- HR for employee conduct issues

## 5) Data Privacy Requests (PDPL aware handling)
If the customer requests:
- access to personal data
- correction or deletion
- objection to automated processing
Then:
- confirm receipt
- request minimal verification through official channels
- follow the internal privacy process
- do not promise an outcome or timeline beyond what policy allows

## 6) Social Media and Press
- all external statements for P0/P1 must be reviewed
- do not disclose internal incident details
- avoid blaming third parties without confirmed evidence

## 7) What Not To Do
- do not provide legal advice to customers
- do not threaten the customer
- do not argue publicly
- do not share confidential investigation details

## 8) How Agent 3 Should Use This Playbook
- reverse prompt should include:
  - safe approved language suggestions
  - evidence preservation checklist
  - approval and escalation routing
  - what not to say
