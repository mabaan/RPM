# Management Playbook

Version: 1.0
Owner: Executive Sponsor (demo)
Applies to: P0 and P1 incidents, cross team coordination, reputational and regulatory risk

## 1) Mission
Coordinate fast, controlled response. Ensure documentation, approvals, and consistent messaging.

## 2) Inputs From Dashboard
- incident summary and evidence
- routed team and watchers
- risk breakdown (virality, compliance, financial, operational)
- guardrails verdict

## 3) Crisis Governance SOP
### Step 1: Confirm priority and activate response
- P0: open war room channel and assign incident commander
- P1: assign owner and daily update cadence

### Step 2: Assign roles
- Incident Commander (single owner)
- Customer Comms Owner (Customer Service)
- Public Comms Owner (Marketing)
- Technical Owner (IT)
- Legal Reviewer (Legal)

### Step 3: Establish single source of truth
- incident log
- timeline updates
- approved messaging snippets

### Step 4: Decide on customer impact actions
- approve goodwill actions only through policy and Finance review
- ensure consistency and fairness

### Step 5: Approvals
- any public statement needs Legal review for P0/P1
- any statement involving security, fraud, or data privacy needs Legal review

### Step 6: Close and learn
- confirm resolution
- request RCA and improvements
- update playbooks based on lessons learned

## 4) Escalation Triggers
Always escalate to Legal:
- regulator mention, press mention, legal threat
- data privacy rights request at scale
- suspected data breach or cyber incident

Always escalate to IT:
- outage, performance degradation, security patterns

Always escalate to Marketing:
- high reach SNS, misinformation trends, coordinated attacks

## 5) What Not To Do
- do not authorize public commitments without Legal
- do not allow conflicting messages across teams
- do not skip documentation

## 6) How Agent 3 Should Use This Playbook
- reverse prompt must include:
  - who owns the incident
  - what decisions are pending
  - approval checklist
  - update cadence recommendation
