# Finance Playbook

Version: 1.0
Owner: Finance Operations Lead (demo)
Applies to: fees, billing disputes, refunds, chargebacks indicators, payment failures, reconciliation issues

## 1) Mission
Investigate financial customer issues accurately, communicate process and timelines clearly, and coordinate approvals through the correct authority.

## 2) Inputs From Dashboard
- incident summary and evidence quotes
- transaction references (masked)
- requested action (refund, fee waiver, investigation)
- priority and reputational risk indicators

## 3) Core Rules
- Do not promise refunds or approvals.
- Do not request sensitive credentials.
- Keep records complete for auditability.
- For suspected fraud or suspicious activity, coordinate with Legal and the financial crime function (if present).

## 4) Billing and Fees SOP
### Step 1: Validate identifiers
- confirm customer identity through the official workflow (via Customer Service)
- confirm transaction reference, date, amount, channel

### Step 2: Check internal systems
- payment logs
- fee schedule mapping
- ledger entries and posting status
- reversals and adjustments history

### Step 3: Classify the issue
- duplicate charge
- incorrect fee
- missing reversal
- pending vs posted confusion
- FX rate concern (if applicable)
- merchant dispute (card related)

### Step 4: Provide an investigation update
To Customer Service:
- what you checked
- what you still need
- estimated timeline (not a promise)

### Step 5: Resolution paths
- correction: posting fix or adjustment request
- refund request: submit for review and approval per policy
- chargeback path: follow scheme process (if applicable) through the official channel

### Step 6: Document
- decision and rationale
- approval chain
- timestamps
- customer impact

## 5) Payment Failure Windows (For Outages)
If there is a confirmed payment disruption:
- record start and end window (approx)
- identify impacted transaction types
- coordinate with IT for root cause confirmation
- provide Customer Service with clear guidance on what customers should do next

## 6) High Risk and Escalation
Escalate to Management:
- high value disputes
- repeated systemic billing errors
- cases with high reach social exposure

Escalate to Legal:
- allegations of fraud, unauthorized transactions at scale
- regulator mention or legal threat
- suspicious activity concerns

## 7) Approved Customer Facing Language (via Customer Service)
- "We are reviewing the billing records and will confirm what we find."
- "If an adjustment is appropriate, it will be processed through our standard review process."

## 8) What Not To Do
- no commitments on outcomes
- no sharing of internal fee policy documents directly to social media
- no speculation about merchant behavior without evidence

## 9) How Agent 3 Should Use This Playbook
- reverse prompt must include:
  - what to check in finance systems
  - what evidence is required for approval
  - safe language for customer updates
  - escalation triggers
